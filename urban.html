Urban@UW

Urban@UW extends understanding of cities—from people, buildings, infrastructure, and energy to economics, policy, culture, art, and nature—beyond individual topics to dynamically interdependent systems, so that we can holistically design and steward vibrant and welcoming cities in which future generations will thrive.

As Urban@UW’s first employee, I developed an identity, and digital communications plan in line with emerging university standards. In the first year I built a web presence complete with database featuring xx scholars & xx labs and research tackling urban related questions. I also developed course materials for the NextSeattle undergraduate urban innovation challenge and served as the mentor to the team that received 2nd place. My role evolved overseeing general communication strategy and developing process documentation to accommodate our growing team. I provided logistical support for the urban environmental justice in a time of climate change symposium, and was the primary urban@uw facilitator for the metro lab big data and human services workshop. Currently I am working as a program coordinator for Urbanlytics Studio, and continuing my work on the OpenSidewalks project under that umbrella.
