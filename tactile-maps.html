Tactile Maps

Under the guidance of the Taskar Center for Accessible Technology and a group of expert users, this ongoing thesis work pairs a participatory design approach together with rule-based, data derived design strategies in the development of tools that can be used to facilitate navigation and a better understanding of the built environment by people with visual impairments. The project pulls data from OpenStreetMap and uses Rhino 3D’s visual scripting plugin, Grasshopper to generate parametric models which are then 3D printed. This rapid prototyping approach allows for user feedback to be quickly incorporated throughout the design process.

Additionally I am partnered with a group of computer science students, that are working to automate the generation of these designs through a web application, with the goal of provide easy access to low cost tactile maps anywhere in the world.
